THE GREAT IMAGINING AFRICA PODCAST.

Background

The great imagining Africa podcast is an initiative of the great imagining Africa and African visual artists associates that is focusing on extending exchange of ideas and conversation about the planetary and ecological emergency in African schools and communities in Uganda and beyond.


We are building confidence, empathy, and curiosity for young creatives, innovators and educators who think differently from us and have the courage to change our minds towards the planetary and ecological emergency towards our societies through inclusion and diversity.

The podcast is also looking at empowerment of social justice and a paradigm shift in the African curriculum that provides a diverse way of learning within students and communities.

The Idea & Scope

Our idea podcast will focus public engagement and science communication activities that engage public audiences with the excitement, importance and relevance of ecological science or enhance the experience and skills of others to communicate ecology with public audiences with diverse age brackets of children of 7-12 years, teenagers aged 13-18 years, and adults of 18 years and above

Our scope will involve visiting schools and communities to streamline a few initiatives and have conversations with initiators and coordinators. This is projected to happen every once a week from 2oth march and once every day within the earth week.

Videos and photographs about a particular idea and conversation will be recorded and stored for evaluation and future purposes on all our platforms.

Podcast Objectives

  • Empower community members to explore subjects for themselves and have experiences in producing think tanks.
  • Encouraging communities and schools to inspire ideas around new ways regarding ecology, science, innovation, and creativity.
  • Empower young people in understanding the eco system and the universe through shared learning.
  • Communicate evidence-based messaging and the use of ecological science as a solution to local and global challenges.
  • Raise awareness and public understanding of ecological careers, their relevance and importance, breadth, and diversity, and show that anybody can become an ecologist.
  • Share how individuals can take action to benefit the environment, themselves, and society.
  • Increase the ability of others to deliver the above aims through outreach and engagement activities.
The podcast aims to Raise awareness through inclusion and diversity in learning about the importance of mother earth and the use of ecological art, science, and imagination as a solution to global challenges.
